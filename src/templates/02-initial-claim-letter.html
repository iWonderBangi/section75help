Your liability under Section 75

Section 75(1) of the Consumer Credit Act 1974 provides that where a debtor has a claim against a supplier in connection with a transaction made under a debtor-creditor-supplier agreement, the creditor is jointly and severally liable with the supplier in respect of that claim.

The conditions of Section 75(1) are satisfied in this case:

  • The purchase was made using a regulated consumer credit agreement (my personal credit card).
  • The total purchase price is £[TOTAL CONTRACT PRICE], which is more than £100 and no more than £30,000, satisfying Section 75(3)(b).
  • The supplier is in breach of contract as described above.

As a party that is jointly and severally liable, you are not entitled to require me to exhaust my remedies against the supplier before pursuing my claim against you. The right to claim against either or both parties simultaneously is a fundamental feature of joint and several liability.
