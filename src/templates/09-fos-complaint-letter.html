Use this document when Your credit card provider has issued a final rejection of your Section 75 claim, or has not responded within eight weeks of your original claim. You must send this to the Financial Ombudsman Service within six months of the date on the bank's final response letter. The FOS service is free and its decisions are binding on the bank.

Complaint Against [CREDIT CARD PROVIDER NAME] — Refusal of Section 75 Claim

Dear Sir or Madam,

I write to refer a complaint to the Financial Ombudsman Service against [CREDIT CARD PROVIDER NAME] (FCA firm reference number: [CREDIT CARD PROVIDER FCA FRN — find this at register.fca.org.uk]) in respect of their refusal of my Section 75 claim under the Consumer Credit Act 1974.

Summary of the complaint

On [DATE OF ORIGINAL PURCHASE], I made a purchase from [SUPPLIER OR COMPANY NAME] for [DESCRIPTION OF GOODS OR SERVICES] at a total price of £[TOTAL CONTRACT PRICE]. I paid £[AMOUNT CHARGED TO CREDIT CARD] of this amount on my [CREDIT CARD PROVIDER NAME] credit card, account ending [LAST FOUR DIGITS].

The supplier subsequently [DESCRIBE THE FAILURE — e.g. "went into administration on [DATE] and failed to deliver the goods or services" / "delivered goods that were not of satisfactory quality" / "delivered goods materially different from their description"]. This constitutes a breach of contract on the part of the supplier.

My claim and the bank's response

I made a formal Section 75 claim to [CREDIT CARD PROVIDER NAME] on [DATE OF ORIGINAL CLAIM LETTER], claiming the sum of £[TOTAL AMOUNT CLAIMED].

[CREDIT CARD PROVIDER NAME] issued their final response on [DATE OF FINAL RESPONSE LETTER], rejecting my claim on the following grounds: [SUMMARISE THE BANK'S STATED REASONS FOR REJECTION].

If you sent a rebuttal letter and the bank rejected that too, add the following sentence — otherwise delete it.

I responded with a written rebuttal on [DATE OF REBUTTAL LETTER], setting out why their rejection was not well-founded. [CREDIT CARD PROVIDER NAME] maintained their rejection by letter dated [DATE OF SECOND REJECTION].

Why I believe the bank's decision is wrong

The conditions of Section 75(1) of the Consumer Credit Act 1974 are clearly satisfied in this case:

  1. The purchase was made using a regulated consumer credit agreement (my personal credit card issued by [CREDIT CARD PROVIDER NAME]).
  2. The total purchase price of £[TOTAL CONTRACT PRICE] is between £100 and £30,000, satisfying Section 75(3)(b).
  3. The supplier is in breach of contract as described above.

[CREDIT CARD PROVIDER NAME] is jointly and severally liable with the supplier under Section 75(1). Their reasons for rejection are: [EXPLAIN BRIEFLY WHY THE BANK'S STATED REASONS ARE WRONG — e.g. "not well-founded in law: joint and several liability does not require me to pursue the supplier first" / "incorrect: the full contract value, not just the card payment, is covered by Section 75" / "contrary to OFT v Lloyds TSB [2007] UKHL 48, which confirmed that Section 75 applies to overseas purchases"].

What I am asking the FOS to do

I ask the Financial Ombudsman Service to investigate my complaint and to direct [CREDIT CARD PROVIDER NAME] to:

  1. Pay me the sum of £[TOTAL AMOUNT CLAIMED] in full settlement of my Section 75 claim.
  2. [ANY ADDITIONAL REMEDY — e.g. "Pay interest on this sum from [DATE] at 8% per annum, consistent with FOS standard practice" — delete if not applicable]

Confirmation of eligibility to complain

  • I am the account holder named on the credit card account.
  • The bank's final response letter is dated [DATE OF FINAL RESPONSE]. I am referring this complaint within six months of that date, satisfying the time limit under DISP 2.8.2R.
  • I have not previously referred this complaint to the FOS.
  • I have not commenced legal proceedings against [CREDIT CARD PROVIDER NAME] in respect of this matter.
