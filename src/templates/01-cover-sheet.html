Step-by-step — most common situation (business failure or non-delivery)

  1. Read Document 4 (Plain English Process Guide) before you do anything else.
  2. Gather your evidence. You will need: your credit card statement showing the transaction; your order confirmation or receipt; evidence of the failure (administration notice, delivery failure, etc.); any correspondence with the supplier.
  3. Complete Document 2 (Initial Claim Letter). Fill in every placeholder marked in bold, yellow-highlighted square brackets. Delete the option paragraphs you do not need.
  4. Complete Document 3 (Evidence Cover Letter). List each document you are enclosing.
  5. Send Documents 2 and 3 together with your evidence by recorded post or via your card provider's secure messaging system. Keep a full copy of everything you send.
  6. Wait up to eight weeks. Your card provider must respond within eight weeks under FCA rules (DISP 1.6.2R). Note the date you sent your claim.
  7. If they pay: done. Confirm the refund appears on your statement.
  8. If they reject: choose the correct rebuttal (Documents 5, 6, 7, or 8 — see the table below) and respond in writing only.
  9. If they reject again, or do not respond within eight weeks: send Document 9 to the Financial Ombudsman Service. You must do this within six months of the date of their final response letter.

Choosing the right rebuttal (Documents 5 to 8)

What the bank said Document to use
Vague rejection, no specific reason given Document 5 — Standard
"You must pursue the retailer first" or "we are not jointly liable" Document 5 — Standard (Option A)
"There is insufficient evidence" Document 5 — Standard (Option B)
"The purchase does not qualify" or they dispute the price threshold Document 5 — Standard (Option C)
No reasoned response provided Document 5 — Standard (Option D)
"Section 75 does not apply to overseas purchases" Document 6 — Overseas Purchase
"Only part of the price was on the card, so we are only liable for that portion" Document 7 — Part Payment
"You paid a booking agent or third party, not the supplier directly" Document 8 — Third Party / Booking Agent
Any rejection — bank has issued a final response letter Document 9 — FOS Complaint Letter

Important notes before you start

  • Do not rely on telephone calls. All Section 75 claims and rebuttals should be in writing. Phone calls are not consistently logged and leave you with no paper trail.
  • Keep copies of everything you send and receive. Note the date of every communication.
  • You do not need a solicitor for a standard Section 75 claim. These templates are designed to be used directly by the account holder.
  • The Financial Ombudsman Service is free to use and its decisions are binding on the bank. A bank rejection is not the end of the road.
  • Time limits matter. You have six months from the date of the bank's final response letter to refer to the FOS. Do not miss this deadline.
