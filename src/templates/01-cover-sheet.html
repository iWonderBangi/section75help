Section 75 Claim Pack

Eight documents for making and pursuing a Section 75 claim under the Consumer Credit Act 1974.

What is in this pack

# Document When to use it
1 Initial Claim Letter Your first letter to your credit card provider. Send this with Document 2 and your evidence.
2 Evidence Cover Letter A cover sheet listing the documents you are enclosing. Always send alongside Document 1.
3 Plain English Process Guide Read before you start. Sets out the full process, realistic timelines, and what the bank is likely to say.
4 Rejection Rebuttal — Standard Bank has rejected without a specific reason, or says you must pursue the retailer first.
5 Rejection Rebuttal — Overseas Purchase Bank says Section 75 does not apply because you bought abroad.
6 Rejection Rebuttal — Part Payment Bank says it is only liable for the portion you paid by credit card, not the full amount.
7 Rejection Rebuttal — Third Party / Booking Agent Bank says Section 75 does not apply because you paid an intermediary, not the supplier directly.
8 FOS Complaint Letter Bank has issued a final rejection. Send this to the Financial Ombudsman Service within six months.

Step-by-step — most common situation (business failure or non-delivery)

  1. Read Document 3 (Plain English Process Guide) before you do anything else.
  2. Gather your evidence. You will need: your credit card statement showing the transaction; your order confirmation or receipt; evidence of the failure (administration notice, delivery failure, etc.); any correspondence with the supplier.
  3. Complete Document 1 (Initial Claim Letter). Fill in every placeholder marked in bold square brackets. Delete the option paragraphs you do not need.
  4. Complete Document 2 (Evidence Cover Letter). List each document you are enclosing.
  5. Send Documents 1 and 2 together with your evidence by recorded post or via your card provider's secure messaging system. Keep a full copy of everything.
  6. Wait up to eight weeks. Your card provider must respond within eight weeks under FCA rules. Note the date you sent your claim.
  7. If they pay: done.
  8. If they reject: choose the correct rebuttal (Documents 4, 5, 6 or 7 — see the table above) and respond in writing.
  9. If they reject again, or do not respond within eight weeks: send Document 8 to the Financial Ombudsman Service. You must do this within six months of the date of their final response letter.

Choosing the right rebuttal (Documents 4 to 7)

What the bank said Document to use
Vague rejection, no specific reason given Document 4 — Standard
"You must pursue the retailer first" or "we are not jointly liable" Document 4 — Standard
"We are only liable for the amount on the card, not the full price" Document 4 — Standard (Option C) or Document 6 — Part Payment
"Section 75 does not apply to overseas purchases" Document 5 — Overseas Purchase
"Only part of the price was on the card, so we are not liable for the full amount" Document 6 — Part Payment
"You paid a booking agent / third party, not the supplier directly" Document 7 — Third Party / Booking Agent
Any rejection, final response letter received Document 8 — FOS Complaint Letter

Important notes

  • Do not rely on telephone calls. All Section 75 claims and rebuttals should be in writing. Phone calls are not logged consistently and leave you with no paper trail.
  • Keep copies of everything you send and receive. Note the date of every communication.
  • You do not need a solicitor for a standard Section 75 claim. These templates are designed to be used directly.
  • The Financial Ombudsman Service is free to use and its decisions are binding on the bank. Do not let a bank rejection be the end of the road.
  • Time limits matter. You have six months from the date of the bank's final response letter to refer to the FOS. Do not miss this deadline.
