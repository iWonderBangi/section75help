Formal Rebuttal of Rejected Section 75 Claim — Consumer Credit Act 1974

Grounds for rebuttal

Option A — "You must pursue the retailer first" or no specific reason given

You have stated that I must exhaust my remedies against the supplier before pursuing my claim against you, or you have rejected my claim without providing a lawful basis for doing so.

Section 75(1) of the Consumer Credit Act 1974 imposes joint and several liability on the creditor. Under joint and several liability, a claimant may pursue any one or all of the jointly liable parties, in any order, without any obligation to exhaust remedies against one before pursuing another. There is no provision in the Consumer Credit Act 1974, nor in any decided case, that requires a Section 75 claimant to pursue the supplier before the creditor. This is especially so where, as here, the supplier has [gone into administration / ceased trading / refused to engage] and pursuit of the supplier is not a realistic or timely remedy.

Option B — "There is insufficient evidence"

You have stated that my claim is not supported by sufficient evidence.

I refer you to the documents submitted with my original claim letter dated [DATE OF ORIGINAL CLAIM], which include: [LIST KEY DOCUMENTS — e.g. "credit card statement dated [DATE]; order confirmation from [SUPPLIER] dated [DATE]; notice of administration dated [DATE]"]. These documents establish: (a) that the transaction was made on my credit card; (b) the total purchase price; (c) the supplier's breach of contract; and (d) the loss I have suffered.

If you require a specific additional document that you consider necessary to determine this claim, please identify what is required and I will endeavour to provide it within 14 days. A general assertion of insufficient evidence, without specifying what is lacking, does not constitute adequate grounds for rejection under DISP 1.6.4R.

Option C — "The purchase does not qualify" or the bank disputes the price threshold

You have stated that my purchase does not meet the qualifying conditions for a Section 75 claim.

The qualifying conditions under Section 75(1) are: (1) the purchase was made under a regulated debtor-creditor-supplier agreement; (2) the cash price of the goods or services was more than £100 and did not exceed £30,000; and (3) the supplier was in breach of contract or guilty of misrepresentation. All three conditions are satisfied: [EXPLAIN BRIEFLY — e.g. "the purchase was made on my personal credit card; the total purchase price was £[AMOUNT], which is within the statutory range; and [SUPPLIER NAME] has failed to deliver the goods or services as set out in my original letter"].

The qualifying threshold refers to the total cash price of the item or service, not the amount charged to the credit card. The requirement that at least £1 is paid on the credit card is satisfied. The full contract price is therefore covered by Section 75.

Option D — Bank has provided no reasoned response

Your letter of [DATE] does not provide specific grounds for rejecting my claim. The Financial Conduct Authority's Dispute Resolution sourcebook (DISP 1.6.4R) requires you to provide a reasoned final response setting out your reasons for rejecting the complaint. A response that does not engage with the merits of the claim does not satisfy this requirement.

Please provide specific grounds for your rejection within 14 days so that I may respond substantively. If you do not do so, I will treat your letter as a final rejection and refer this matter to the Financial Ombudsman Service.

My position

My Section 75 claim under the Consumer Credit Act 1974 is well-founded. The conditions of Section 75(1) are met. The supplier is in breach of contract. Your organisation is jointly and severally liable for my loss of £[TOTAL AMOUNT CLAIMED].

I ask that you issue a revised final response within 14 days of this letter. If you maintain your rejection, or do not respond within that period, I will refer this matter to the Financial Ombudsman Service without further notice. The FOS has jurisdiction over Section 75 complaints and its determinations are binding on your organisation under DISP 3.6.
