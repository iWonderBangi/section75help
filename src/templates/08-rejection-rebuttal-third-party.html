I set out below why this rejection is not well-founded on the facts of my transaction.

The debtor-creditor-supplier link

Section 75 requires a "debtor-creditor-supplier agreement" as defined in Section 12 of the Consumer Credit Act 1974. In cases involving booking agents or intermediaries, the question is whether the party that received the credit card payment was the supplier of the goods or services within the meaning of the Act.

Where a booking agent or platform contracts with a consumer as a principal — that is, it sells the product or service in its own name and bears the contractual liability for delivery — it is the supplier for Section 75 purposes. The underlying provider (the hotel, airline, venue, or manufacturer) is not the relevant "supplier" in this analysis; the party that entered the contract with the consumer is.

Conversely, where an agent acts in a disclosed agency capacity — that is, it makes clear that it is merely facilitating a contract between the consumer and a named third party — the underlying provider may be the relevant supplier, and whether the three-party link is established depends on the specific contractual structure.

My transaction — the agent acted as principal

Use this section if the agent sold the product in its own name. Delete if not applicable.

[BOOKING AGENT OR INTERMEDIARY NAME] contracted with me as a principal. My contract was with [BOOKING AGENT OR INTERMEDIARY NAME], not with [UNDERLYING SUPPLIER IF KNOWN]. Evidence of this includes: [DESCRIBE THE EVIDENCE — e.g. "my booking confirmation is issued by [AGENT NAME] and bears their company number and registered address" / "the terms and conditions presented at the point of sale state that the contract is between me and [AGENT NAME]" / "my receipt and all correspondence is from [AGENT NAME] and makes no disclosure of a named third-party principal"].

As [BOOKING AGENT OR INTERMEDIARY NAME] was the party with which I contracted and to which I paid, and as I paid on my credit card, the debtor-creditor-supplier link under Section 12(b) of the Consumer Credit Act 1974 is satisfied. Section 75 applies to my claim against you as the creditor.

Alternative — the agent acted on behalf of the underlying supplier

Use this alternative section only if the agent was acting as a disclosed agent for a named third party, and your claim is therefore against the underlying supplier. Delete if not applicable.

Even if [BOOKING AGENT OR INTERMEDIARY NAME] was acting as a disclosed agent for [UNDERLYING SUPPLIER NAME], the debtor-creditor-supplier link may still be established. Where credit-card payments made to an agent are passed to the underlying supplier in settlement of the consumer's obligation, the economic substance of the transaction is that the credit financed the purchase from the underlying supplier. The Financial Ombudsman Service has upheld Section 75 claims in analogous circumstances where the economic reality of the transaction establishes the required link.
